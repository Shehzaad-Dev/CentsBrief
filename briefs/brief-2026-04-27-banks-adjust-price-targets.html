Wells Fargo has cut its price target for Southern Copper, a significant player in the copper industry, despite the ongoing copper rally. This move may seem counterintuitive given the current demand for copper, which has been driven by the growth of the renewable energy sector, electric vehicles, and infrastructure development.

Copper is a critical component in the production of solar panels, wind turbines, and electrical wiring, and its demand is expected to continue to rise in the coming years. However, Wells Fargo's decision to cut the price target for Southern Copper suggests that the bank believes the company's growth may be hindered by factors such as increased competition from other copper producers, operational challenges, or declining ore grades.

On the other hand, Cantor Fitzgerald has raised its price target for Strategy, indicating a more optimistic outlook for the company's future performance. Strategy has been investing heavily in digital transformation, artificial intelligence, and data analytics, which may enable the company to improve its operational efficiency, enhance customer experience, and expand its market share.

Meanwhile, JPMorgan has lowered its price target for Everpure to $80, citing an upcoming AI-driven demand outlook that could impact the company's valuation.
